# 10 AI-first companies that should be using Topify.ai but likely are not

Checked on 2026-04-15 using public official websites.

Important note:
- The statement "should be using Topify but are not" is an inference from public evidence.
- What I mean is: I found no public sign on these sites that AI-answer visibility is being operationalized through dedicated GEO / AEO tooling, and the public site structure suggests clear room for answer-engine optimization.

## Selection logic

I looked for AI-first companies that:

- depend on discovery through AI-adjacent buying journeys
- sell products people actively ask ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, or Perplexity about
- already invest in product marketing, content, or community
- still show gaps in comparison content, answer-ready pages, or AI-visibility positioning

## 1. Lovable

- Website: https://lovable.dev
- What they do: AI app builder for creating apps and websites by chatting with AI
- Why they need AI search optimization: developers and non-technical founders increasingly ask AI tools for "best AI app builder" and "Bolt vs Lovable vs Replit" style recommendations
- Decision maker title: Head of Growth or VP Marketing
- What they are currently doing wrong:
- The homepage clearly explains the product, but the public structure looks creation-led rather than evaluation-led
- I found community, pricing, templates, guides, and FAQs, but no visible comparison-led wedge on the main site navigation
- That means Lovable risks losing decision-stage AI prompts where buyers want alternatives, tradeoffs, and category framing

## 2. Bolt

- The homepage is strong on product excitement, design systems, and builder roles, but weaker on structured answer-engine capture

1. Lovable
2. Bolt
3. Vapi
4. Gamma
5. Clay
6. Replit
7. HeyGen
8. Synthesia

## Why this order

- Lovable, Bolt, and Vapi are in fast-moving categories with heavy prompt-driven discovery and obvious room for comparison-page expansion
- Gamma and Clay have strong product-market attention, but broad public messaging that can leave AI-answer capture on the table
- Replit, HeyGen, and Synthesia have scale, which means even small AI-visibility gains can turn into meaningful pipeline impact
- ElevenLabs is huge, but the brand is already strong enough that the opportunity is less about awareness and more about segmentation precision
