# Two landing page headline and subheadline options

Checked on 2026-04-15.

## Version A: Benefit-focused

**Headline:** Turn Hard Work Into Visible Progress  
**Subheadline:** Build healthier habits with smart coaching, personalized workout plans, and adaptive guidance that helps you stay consistent without burning out.

**Why this could work**

- It centers the outcome users want most: visible progress they can feel and sustain.
- "Hard Work" acknowledges effort, while "Visible Progress" promises reward, which reduces skepticism.
- The subheadline stacks three concrete benefits: personalization, smart coaching, and sustainable consistency.
- Psychologically, this version appeals to aspiration, self-efficacy, and identity-based motivation. It frames the product as a partner in becoming the kind of person who follows through.

## Version B: Pain-point-focused

**Headline:** Stop Starting Over Every Monday  
**Subheadline:** FitMind AI creates realistic workout plans, adjusts to your energy and schedule, and helps you stay on track when motivation drops.

**Why this could work**

- The headline names a familiar frustration in plain language, which creates instant recognition.
- "Starting over every Monday" is emotionally vivid and signals the product understands the user's real problem, not just the category.
- The subheadline reduces anxiety by promising realistic plans and adaptation instead of intensity or guilt.
- Psychologically, this version works through problem recognition, emotional resonance, and loss aversion. It offers relief from the exhausting cycle of inconsistency.

## Recommendation

If the audience is already fitness-aware but skeptical of generic apps, Version B may produce stronger immediate attention because it sounds more human and specific. If the audience is warmer and more solution-seeking, Version A is likely to convert better because it feels optimistic and progress-oriented.
