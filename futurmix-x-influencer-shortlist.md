# FuturMix X influencer shortlist

FuturMix positions itself as a unified AI gateway for GPT, Claude, Gemini, and Seedance with resilient routing and enterprise observability. The shortlist below was optimized for buyer fit, not raw follower count.

## Selection method

- Prioritized X accounts in roughly the 10K to 200K range with real AI builder, ML engineer, CTO, founder, and devtool audiences
- Favored people who regularly discuss agent infrastructure, model routing, evals, developer tools, or shipping production AI products
- Used public snapshots from X and third-party audience tools where available

## Top 5 accounts

## 1. @swyx (Shawn Wang)

- Followers: about 152K
- Content focus: AI engineering, developer tools, agent workflows, model tooling commentary, Latent Space / AI Engineer ecosystem
- Why he fits: one of the strongest pure-overlap audiences for FuturMix because his followers are builders choosing real production stacks
- Pitch angle: `Ship against GPT, Claude, Gemini, and Seedance from one endpoint. Add failover and observability without building custom provider glue.`
- Best format: benchmark thread plus a short demo clip showing provider failover and traces

## 2. @hwchase17 (Harrison Chase)

- Followers: about 90K
- Content focus: LangChain, LangGraph, agents, orchestration, observability, production AI systems
- Why he fits: his audience already wrestles with multi-model orchestration, reliability, and production visibility
- Pitch angle: `Use FuturMix under LangGraph-style agent systems to simplify routing, retries, failover, and model switching.`
- Best format: technical integration walkthrough or co-built example app

## 3. @jerryjliu0 (Jerry Liu)

- Followers: about 71K
- Content focus: LlamaIndex, RAG, OCR, enterprise AI workflows, production retrieval systems
- Why he fits: strong audience overlap for document-heavy and enterprise AI workflows where model flexibility and observability matter
- Pitch angle: `Compare GPT, Claude, and Gemini inside production document workflows without rewriting provider-specific code each time.`
- Best format: workflow case study or code example tied to RAG / OCR / enterprise pipelines

## 4. @simonw (Simon Willison)

- Followers: about 122K
- Content focus: hands-on LLM experiments, open-source tooling, benchmarks, evals, implementation notes, model behavior analysis
- Why he fits: his audience trusts artifact-first reviews, which makes him useful for credibility with senior builders
- Pitch angle: `Here is a reproducible test harness showing how one gateway handles multi-provider prompts, outages, and observability across providers.`
- Best format: credits plus a real notebook or demo optimized for reviewability, not ad copy

## 5. @bentossell (Ben Tossell)

- Followers: about 189K
- Content focus: AI tools, startup builders, dev tools, no-code / AI product discovery, operator audience
- Why he fits: strongest pick for distribution and discovery among founders and builders actively trying new tooling
- Pitch angle: `One API to access GPT, Claude, Gemini, and Seedance with reliability features built in, so startups can ship faster without vendor lock-in.`
- Best format: sponsored thread plus product demo

## Best campaign framing for FuturMix

- Lead with production pain, not model list
- Strongest message: `multi-model routing + failover + observability for real AI apps`
- Show a side-by-side demo, an outage or failover scenario, and traces or logs
- Offer test credits so creators can publish something real instead of generic sponsorship copy

If budget is tight, start with swyx, Harrison, and Jerry. That trio is the cleanest match for developer-led adoption.
